Q1. What excites you most about Builder and/or this role?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The thing that most intrigues me about working for an AI-first company is the moment you first really understand what one of these products can do. Something that used to take hours of painstaking work suddenly happens from a single well-written prompt, and you sit there a bit stunned at what just got compressed. I've had that moment with Claude Code, with Lovable, with Figma Make. Each time it shifts what I think is possible.

That feeling is what I want to be closer to in my day-to-day. Builder is one of the few products that consistently delivers it for the design-to-production gap, which is also a gap I've lived in. Selling and supporting something I genuinely react to like that is a much easier job than selling something I don't.

Q2. How do you use AI in your daily role as a Customer Success Manager?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

A few things I actually use, day to day:

- I built an internal CRM on Lovable to manage MV&Co's pipeline, deal stages and client comms. It replaced a spreadsheet plus three browser tabs.

- I prototype client website components in Figma Make, then ship and iterate them in VS Code with Claude Code. The design-to-code loop is genuinely faster, but I still review every line. Accepting AI output uncritically is how you ship subtle bugs that compile fine.

- I have meeting recordings and transcripts auto-routed into client-specific folders in Google Drive, so when I walk into a call I have the right context already surfaced rather than scrambling for it.

- For account work like research, segmentation, outbound personalisation and first-draft client comms, I use a mix of Claude and ChatGPT. Treat it as a starting point, not a finished deliverable. The taste call is still mine.

The honest version: AI hasn't changed what good account management is. It's removed a lot of the admin around it, which means I spend more time actually thinking about the customer.
